OTP Email Verification Requirement

Effective Date: 13 April 2026 (all new magic links from this date onward require OTP before Gift Aid form display)

Why OTP?

Privacy & Security: Magic link allows anyone with the QR code to access the donation record (including amount). OTP adds email ownership verification before exposing donor data.
HMRC Requirement (Inferred): HMRC guidance emphasizes explicit donor consent + identity verification. OTP provides audit trail that donor verified their email address before claim.
Fraud Prevention: Reduces risk of someone else scanning the QR and attempting Gift Aid claim on someone else's donation.

OTP Flow (New)

1

Donor lands on magic link page

Frontend displays: "Enter your email to verify and access your Gift Aid form." Email input field (pre-filled from donation if available, but modifiable).

2

Send OTP email

Backend Cloud Function generates random 6-digit code. Stores in temporary cache (10-minute expiry). Sends email: "Your SwiftCause verification code is: XXXXXX. This code expires in 10 minutes."

3

Donor enters OTP

Frontend displays code input field (6 digits). Real-time validation as donor types. Auto-submit on 6th digit entered.

4

Backend validates OTP

POST /api/magic-link/verify-otp receives {token, email, code}. Verifies: code matches cache entry + not expired + not reused. Returns {verified: true} or error.

5

Gift Aid form displayed

Only after OTP verified, frontend renders Gift Aid form pre-filled with donation data. Form shows email (with option to edit if different from OTP-verified address).

Postcode Validation for Gift Aid Eligibility

Effective Date: 13 April 2026 (go-live requirement)

Background: Why Postcode Matters

HMRC Rule: Only UK-resident donors are eligible for Gift Aid relief (20% claim). Postcode is the primary (cheapest) way to verify UK residency.
Non-UK Processing: Donations from non-UK postcodes are still accepted and recorded, but flagged as ineligible for Gift Aid claim. These must be excluded from HMRC R68 monthly export batches.

Validation Logic

  1. Donor enters postcode on Gift Aid form (or pre-filled from record).
  2. Backend validates postcode format: UK postcodes are patterns like "SW1A 1AA" or "M1 1AE". Regex: ^[A-Z]{1,2}\d[A-Z\d]? ?\d[A-Z]{2}$ (case-insensitive).
  3. Query against HMRC postcode database: SwiftCause obtains official HMRC postcode file (free, public). Store locally in Firestore or cache for O(1) lookup. Postcode either exists in database (eligible) or not (ineligible / international).
  4. Store eligibility flag: Gift Aid declaration record includes postcodeEligible: true/false.
  5. Export segregation: HMRC R68 monthly export only includes declarations where postcodeEligible == true.

Example Scenarios

"M1 1AE" (Manchester) Valid UK postcode Yes Included in monthly R68 export

Apple Pay / Google Pay Integration for Tap-to-Pay

Priority: P1 (high-impact volume driver, soft deadline 1 May)

Opportunity

Volume Spike Expected: Apple Pay + Google Pay adoption in UK exceeded 60% for adult smartphone users (2024 data). Enabling phone-based tap-to-pay on kiosks will increase donation conversions by ~15–25% pre-launch.
Kiosk Hardware Prerequisite: NFC reader in SwiftCause kiosks must support Apple Pay (NFC-A, Type 2) + Google Pay (Tap & Pay protocol). Most modern Android tablets + iPad models support this out-of-the-box; older devices may not.
Stripe Integration: Stripe Terminal (existing Stripe integration) supports Apple Pay + Google Pay if kiosk hardware is compatible. No API changes; config flag only.

Inventory all deployed SwiftCause kiosks. Check NFC capability + Android/iOS versions. Document which devices can support Apple/Google Pay out-of-the-box vs require firmware upgrade.

For devices needing OS update, coordinate with hardware vendor. Test NFC tap on trial device with Stripe Terminal test credentials. Document APK changes required (if Android).

24–28 Apr — Stripe Terminal Config

Enable Apple Pay + Google Pay on Stripe Terminal reader (settings in Stripe Dashboard). Test full donation flow: tap Apple Pay → Stripe processes → donation recorded. Repeat for Google Pay.

1 May — Rollout to All Kiosks

Push firmware updates + Stripe Terminal configs to all live kiosks. Train ops staff on dual-payment acceptance (card tap vs Apple/Google Pay tap). Monitor payment success rate for issues.

9 May — Go-Live with Apple/Google Pay Active

Risk Mitigation

If hardware incompatible: Kiosks without NFC support will continue to accept physical credit cards (contactless or chip). No donor flow disruption, but opportunity cost of not capturing Apple/Google Pay volume (estimated £3–5k over first 3 months).
Soft Launch Option: If Apple/Google Pay integration slips past 1 May, SwiftCause can still launch with contactless card-only (not ideal, but viable). Apple/Google Pay can be added in Month 2 post-launch with firmware update to all kiosks (lower risk than trying to rush before go-live).

HMRC Compliance Framework

R68 Monthly Export Schedule

Monthly Submission: Gift Aid declarations must be exported to HMRC monthly using R68 format. Submission deadline: 19th of following month (or 20th if 19th is weekend).
Tax Year Boundary (April 6): HMRC tax year resets on April 6 each calendar year. SwiftCause export batches must be segregated by tax year (e.g., Apr 6 2026 – Apr 5 2027 = batch 1; Apr 6 2027 – Apr 5 2028 = batch 2).
Eligibility Segregation: R68 export file must exclude all donations with postcodeEligible: false. Non-eligible donations logged separately in audit table (not submitted to HMRC, but retained for compliance review).
