4. Product Positioning Note

This doesn't make SwiftCause a "kiosk" The tablet-in-stand is an optional premium accessory — one of many collection points. A charity might have one floor stand at reception AND 10 volunteers' phones AND 50 QR posters around the building. The stand is an anchor point, not the product. Position it as: "Put SwiftCause at your front door — then put it everywhere else too."

Suggested product naming to keep messaging consistent:

Don't sayDo say
"SwiftCause kiosk""SwiftCause Collection Point" or "SwiftCause Welcome Stand"
"Kiosk hardware""Collection stand" or "Venue stand"
"Our kiosk solution""Fixed collection point — one of many ways to collect"

8. Suggested Next Steps

Week 1 Decide on bundle. Pick Option A (budget Android) for volume or Option B/D for demos. You can mix: Android for charity rollout, iPad for your demo kit.
Week 1–2 Order 1 test unit. Buy one tablet + stand combo. Test SwiftCause web app in kiosk mode. Verify: screen brightness, viewing angle, lock security, power cable routing.
Week 2–3 Test kiosk lockdown. Set up Samsung Knox or Apple Guided Access. Confirm the donation flow works end-to-end. Stress test with team.
Week 3–4 Photography & pricing. Photograph the stand setup for marketing materials. Set a resale or rental price if offering to charities. Create a "Collection Stand" product page.
Week 4+ Bulk order. Based on test results and early customer interest, order initial batch (5–10 units). Negotiate bulk pricing with Samsung UK or Amazon Business.

Decision needed from Qamar

Three questions to unlock the next step:

  1. Budget flex? Are you firm at £200 or willing to stretch to £230 for the 11″ Android sweet spot?
  2. Screen size priority? 8.7″ (cheap, portable) vs 11″ (better for public-facing)?
  3. Mixed fleet? Happy to use Android for charity rollout + iPad for your own demo kit? Or need one platform only?
